# Skill 21: Enterprise Transition Design

## Purpose

Move an organization from product, channel, or department silos toward customer-centered operations through practical software rollout.

## When To Use

- Planning CRM transformation.
- Migrating legacy CRM processes.
- Introducing customer-centric workflows.

## Inputs

- Current systems.
- Siloed workflows.
- Pilot opportunities.
- Change constraints.
- Target operating model.

## Repeatable Steps

1. Identify the smallest CRM pilot that proves customer-centric value.
2. Build around real customer workflows, not abstract platform capability.
3. Integrate with existing systems where full replacement is not realistic.
4. Expand from pilot to adjacent teams and processes.
5. Use metrics and feedback to guide rollout.

## Software Artifacts

- Transition roadmap.
- Pilot CRM module.
- Migration plan.
- Integration map.
- Rollout scorecard.

## Acceptance Checks

- The transition has a measurable pilot.
- Legacy constraints are explicitly handled.
- The rollout improves customer outcomes, not just system adoption.
- Each phase increases customer data or workflow coherence.
