# Skill 01: Customer Strategy Framing

## Purpose

Define what customer value the CRM system is meant to create before designing features.

## When To Use

- Starting a CRM product, module, or major refactor.
- Deciding which customer problems the system should prioritize.
- Aligning sales, service, marketing, product, and operations.

## Inputs

- Business goals.
- Target customer groups.
- Current customer pain points.
- Desired customer outcomes.
- Existing CRM metrics and gaps.

## Repeatable Steps

1. Name the customer value the system should increase.
2. Define which customers matter most and why.
3. Identify the relationship behavior the system should encourage.
4. Translate strategy into measurable CRM outcomes.
5. Reject features that do not support customer value, trust, or relationship learning.

## Software Artifacts

- CRM product strategy.
- Customer outcome map.
- Customer value hypotheses.
- CRM success metrics.
- Prioritized capability roadmap.

## Acceptance Checks

- The feature has a clear customer-value reason to exist.
- The customer is the primary design unit.
- Success can be measured beyond activity volume.
- The design improves either value, trust, learning, or experience.
