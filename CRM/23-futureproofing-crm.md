# Skill 23: Futureproofing CRM

## Purpose

Design CRM architecture so trust, culture, innovation, and customer learning can evolve over time.

## When To Use

- Choosing architecture patterns.
- Designing extensible customer data.
- Planning long-lived CRM platforms.
- Preparing for new channels, AI, or regulations.

## Inputs

- Current CRM architecture.
- Expected growth areas.
- Regulatory risk.
- Data model constraints.
- Integration roadmap.

## Repeatable Steps

1. Keep customer identity and consent stable across changing channels.
2. Use event-driven history where customer learning matters.
3. Keep rules, models, and workflows configurable.
4. Design integration boundaries around customer capabilities.
5. Review architecture against future trust, privacy, and personalization needs.

## Software Artifacts

- Modular CRM architecture.
- Event schema.
- Extensible customer profile.
- Integration contracts.
- Architecture decision records.

## Acceptance Checks

- New channels can attach to the customer model.
- New rules or treatments do not require deep rewrites.
- Customer history survives system changes.
- Trust and privacy controls evolve with the platform.
