# Skill 10: Trust Engineering

## Purpose

Design CRM capabilities that earn and preserve customer trust.

## When To Use

- Building personalization.
- Requesting customer data.
- Designing customer-facing controls.
- Using AI or automated decisions in CRM.

## Inputs

- Sensitive data uses.
- Customer expectations.
- Consent and preference rules.
- Automated decisions.
- Risk scenarios.

## Repeatable Steps

1. Identify what the customer must trust the system to do.
2. Make data requests proportional to customer benefit.
3. Explain important personalization or decisions.
4. Provide controls for consent, preferences, and correction.
5. Detect trust-breaking behaviors such as surprise use, over-contact, or irrelevant treatment.

## Software Artifacts

- Trust risk register.
- Customer-facing data controls.
- Explanation messages.
- Consent enforcement.
- Trust-sensitive event logging.

## Acceptance Checks

- Customers can understand important data uses.
- Personalization does not feel hidden or manipulative.
- Trust controls are enforced technically.
- Employees cannot bypass critical customer promises.
